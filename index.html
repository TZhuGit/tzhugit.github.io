School Projects
At Western University, I have always enjoyed participating in the hackathons and capture the flag events with other computer scientists and developers. The excitement of going to one of these events to connect with people with similar interest within the coding world outside of my classes is exilerating. These events have help me not only learn new development tools but also allow me to develop new problem solving skills through others or good old trail and error. Many sponsor representatives are extremely helpful and friendly, by talking to them they gave me a eye-opening view on the different niches in the technological industry from the known big developing companies such as Google and Amazon to coporate IT consulting powerhouses such as Deloitte and to specialized security hacker firms.

Personal Projects
The two major coding projects I have been apart of are my Gym Trainer android application and House Mate web application. I've always enjoyed designing and contemplating how I can develop applications to tackle daily inconvinences. I'd remember constantly having to think back during each gym session to what numbers I performed last week, how many reps and what weight did I use. I ended up writing them down on my phone's built in notes application and I figured how hard could it be to design an application to make organizing this data more presentable and user-friendly. And then right away at the next hackathon I attended I began learning Android development. Then web application development project came along and I was introduced to a bunch of new terminology and developer tools that gave me a new apprication for all the software I used every day. I worked with 8 other members on a web application to allow people who live together to tackle common problems in an application management tool. The application is designed to monitor and keep track of responsibilities, bills, supply levels and other tasks for each individual within the house.
